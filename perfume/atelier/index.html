Creative discipline

Every decision reduces noise and increases presence.

The atelier approach starts with materials that hold their shape. Citrus is crisp, spice is clean, woods are dry, and sweetness is used only when it adds contrast.

That discipline makes the perfumes feel premium without drifting into excess. The result is a house language that stays coherent across the entire collection.

"A fragrance house should feel as carefully edited as a tailored wardrobe."
OBSIDIAN's design principle

Atelier standards

The presentation is part of the product.

The bottle, typography, and color palette all reinforce the same mood: dark, polished, and confident. That keeps the brand recognizable even before the fragrance is smelled.

Materials

Filtered into a narrow, expressive palette.

The compositions use citrus, spice, woods, leather, and resin in a way that preserves clarity.

Bottle design

Minimal forms, high contrast, no unnecessary decoration.

The visual system leans on black glass, gold detailing, and a clean silhouette to signal luxury.

Finishing

Presented to feel ready the moment it arrives.

Gift notes, protective packaging, and a refined visual hierarchy make each order feel complete.

Sourcing

Materials chosen for character, not noise.

The house favors notes that keep their shape and read clearly on skin. That makes the collection feel deliberate rather than crowded.

Batching

Small-batch feel with a consistent signature.

Every fragrance is arranged to keep the same profile across the lineup while still having a distinct mood and level of intensity.

Packaging

Premium from first glance to final unboxing.

The bottle design and digital presentation stay aligned so the house feels cohesive in production.

Identity

Dark, modern, and quietly assertive.

OBSIDIAN is meant to feel editorial without losing usability. The result is a brand system that can scale while remaining recognizable.
